Places you MUST visit in Liverpool

Albert Docks

The iconic Royal Albert Dock Liverpool is one of the prime locations to visit during your stay at Liverpool.Take advantage of the impressive retail and leisure offering at the Dock,world class museums and attractions and some of the city’s finest independents. Nothing beats a nice walk at the Albert Docks!

Anfield

Even for a non Liverpool supporter a visit to one of the most famous stadiums in europe is always worth a visit. Liverpool Football Club also gives the chance to fans and tourists to take a tour into the stadium and learn about the history of the club. Anfield is definetely worth your time!

Museum of Liverpool

The Museum of Liverpool reflects the city's global significance through its unique geography, history and culture. You can explore how the port, its people, their creative and sporting history have shaped the city.

Sefton Park

Sefton Park in Liverpool is arguably the most known and loved location by the locals. This magnificent 200-acre Park looks more like a natural landscape rather than a man-made park. In spring the sight of millions of golden daffodils around the lake draws residents from across the city and carpets of bluebells give an impression of rural permanence. There is no better place to be on a sunny day!

Beatles Story

The Beatles had a big part to play in Liverpool's history. Be transported on an incredible immersive journey as you go through the story of how four young lads from Liverpool were propelled to the dizzy heights of fame and fortune from their humble childhood beginnings. Replicas of the Casbah, Mathew Street, Abbey Road Studios and The Cavern authentically capture the early '60s, allowing you to personally experience the very places that helped make The Beatles the greatest band in the world.

Liverpool Cathedral

Liverpool Cathedral is another MUST visit place in our beautiful city.Spot all of Liverpool’s great landmarks as you take in a breathtaking 360˚ view from the rooftop of the highest Cathedral in the UK standing at 152m above sea level, two lifts and 108 stairs and on a good clear day you will be able to see the Blackpool Tower!
